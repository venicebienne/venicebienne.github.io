Frank Abagnale Jr. Is A Normal Teenager Who Lived out Everyone's Dreams

The 2002 film directed by Steven Spielberg is an eye-opening biopicture that takes you through different parts of the life Frank Abagnale Jr. From the young age of 16, he embarks on an unusual journey after his parents get a divorce. He runs away from home and starts to forge bank checks to live off of five-dollar meals and to stay in one-star motels.

The first career he fakes is being a pilot. Surprisingly, he was able to pull it off, and even once or twice lead a flight himself. Months later, he visited a hospital and met a nurse who he caught feelings for. He then decided to switch his career, still through illegal means, to become a doctor. He gets engaged to the nurse, Brenda, and they hold an engagement party. Chaos went on as the detective who has been tracking Frank for months, found him in Brenda's parents' house. From this point on, nothing but chaos happens in the film, and it is definitely something you shouldn't miss out on.

Now, what makes our protagonist a misunderstood character? First off, he is a child of divorce. This was the driving force that led him to do all kinds of things just to bring his family back together. It absolutely broke him when he saw his mother marry to a new man. Aside from this, being a neglected child led him to become an overly-independent personality. Lastly, as he grew up, the realities of life hit him like a truck one by one. He realized that life was not as wonderful as his father showed it to be. Though he can be interpreted as an anti-hero protagonist, Frank Abagnale Jr. is like any other coming-of-age main character who just has a bigger motive.
